The challenge

A few words about the Erlang project.

Erlang contacted us for web design, responsive web design, and front-end development for the company's new website. This wasn't a small challenge. The client's PM, Magdalena, was a strongly design-oriented person who wanted to achieve the perfect balance between professionalism and creativity in the organization. We were working on a design with their London office and deliver our code, documentation, and tools to an IT company in Argentina, which then implement our code and complete the back-end development.

Design-oriented client

The client was in search of a perfect balance between professionalism and creativity. Thus, we worked to create a high-quality design and pixel perfect interactions to meet the needs of this demanding client.

Multi-team development

We are delivering front-end code, documentation, and tools to a second IT company and will work with them on front-end and back-end integration.

Multi-location cooperation

London: decisions & feedback
Krakow: web design & front-end coding
Buenos Aires: back-end development & implementation

The solution

1. Project process

We created a safety path for meeting the client's high expectations and short deadline.

To provide a top-quality design while also meeting the client's high expectations within a short timeframe, we prepared a dedicated process. See the work scheme that we built for this project below.

2. Cooperation with the client

We collected the best tools for communication, feedback, and task management in order to make our cooperation with the client as efficient as possible.

The client, who wanted to push the pixels to the limit, required the appropriate tools for collaboration and feedback management. That's why we chose inVision to gather communication and feedback in a maximally visual way with the option of using it later to manage other tasks.

3. Multi-team development

Front-end/Back-end integration & communication

Having separate development teams work on one project is always a challenge. Therefore, for smooth cooperation and the best results after implementation, we selected GitHub as a common ground, inVision for information, and a static HTML prototype for previews and double-checking.
